Philosophy

Good technology should serve the work, not generate more of it. The right system runs in the background, takes the admin off your desk, and stays out of the way while you do the actual job.

Most of the tools small operators pay for aren't broken. They're underused. Software bought without a plan to implement it becomes another subscription line on the credit card statement. I look at what you already have before suggesting anything new.

I'm based in Zell am See and I work with businesses across the valley. The mountain doesn't care about your booking system, but the guests it brings you do. The job, as I see it, is putting the right tool in the right place, configuring it properly, and then getting out of the way.

Digital Foundations
+
A structured audit and implementation sprint for businesses that know they're behind on the digital side and want it fixed properly. The usual entry point.

We go through what's actually in use — accounts, devices, files, email, the workarounds your team has built up over time. I document the current state, fix what's worth fixing in a focused sprint, and hand back a clean foundation you can run yourself.

Website
+
A website you actually own. Clean, fast, no monthly bloat.

A lot of small tourism businesses are stuck in expensive agency retainers or template platforms they can't manage themselves. This is the honest alternative. I design, build, and hand over the keys. When you want to change a phone number, you don't have to call anyone.

Automated Systems
+
Operational efficiency, built around how your business already runs.

If your day runs on shared spreadsheets, group chats and processes only held in someone's head, there's usually an automation that pays for itself inside a fortnight. I map how you actually work, find the slow seams, and put something quiet in place to handle them — using tools your team already knows.

Custom Build
+
Bespoke software, scoped per project. The top of the ladder.

Sometimes the right tool doesn't exist yet, or the one that does is the wrong shape. We scope it together, I build it, and it ships with documentation thorough enough that anyone could pick it up if you ever needed them to.
