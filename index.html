Technology should serve you, not demand your attention. Any good piece of software, any system worth having, should simplify something — free you up, make you more present, remove the administrative weight that comes with running a modern life or business.

There's no point in a new phone, a subscription, a piece of software — personal or professional — if it isn't actually being used, or understood, or working for you. People buy tools without knowing how to use them. That's not a failure of the person. It's a failure of the setup.

I look for technical solutions across the full spectrum of your digital life — to make things flow, reduce the headache, and help you actually enjoy what you own.
